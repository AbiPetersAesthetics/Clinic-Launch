RESEARCH TO BAKE INTO THE PAGE
Use these researched points throughout the content:

1. Aesthetics franchising exists, but the safest models are usually either:
   - lower clinical complexity models such as facials, skin, laser, memberships and retail
   - tightly controlled partnership/JV models
   - company-owned multi-site models

2. Examples to mention in a “Market Examples” section:
   - Laser Clinics UK / Laser Clinics Australia: franchise/partnership style model, often closer to a 50:50 partnership than a light-touch franchise.
   - FACE FOUNDRIÉ: US facial/skin bar franchise, membership-led, lower clinical complexity.
   - GLO30: skincare membership/franchise model.
   - dermani MEDSPA: US medspa franchise with medical supervision.
   - BodyBrite: laser/beauty franchise with lower-cost formats.
   - Thérapie Clinic: not a franchise, but a useful company-owned multi-site comparator.

3. UK regulation is fragmented and tightening:
   - England does not currently regulate many non-surgical cosmetic treatments through CQC when they are purely cosmetic, but that does not mean low risk.
   - CQC applies where regulated activities are carried out, such as surgical procedures or treatment of disease, disorder or injury.
   - England is moving toward a licensing regime for non-surgical cosmetic procedures.
   - Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland may have different rules, especially around laser/IPL and non-surgical cosmetic procedures.
   - Any future franchise model must have nation-specific compliance checks.

4. Medicines and prescribing risks:
   - Prescription-only medicines must not be advertised directly to the public.
   - Avoid product-led marketing around Botox/botulinum toxin.
   - Marketing should use service-led language such as “anti-wrinkle consultations” or “lines and wrinkles treatment options”.
   - Nurse/midwife prescribers are required to consult face-to-face before prescribing elective non-surgical cosmetic medicines from June 2025.
   - A franchise model must not rely on remote prescribing shortcuts.

5. Clinical governance must be central:
   - approved prescribers
   - treatment authority matrix
   - incident reporting
   - complication pathways
   - consent process
   - record keeping
   - audit rights
   - approved suppliers
   - batch/lot traceability
   - insurance checks
   - complaint handling
   - mandatory training and re-certification

6. A better scale route may be:
   - company-owned Winchester proof site
   - second company-owned or closely controlled proof site
   - then pilot a JV/partnership unit
   - only then consider franchise rollout

7. ClinicSurge OS may actually be the higher-value scalable asset:
   - GHL/CRM setup
   - lead handling
   - automations
   - patient conversion
   - reporting
   - marketing systems
   - launch planning
   - retention workflows
   - this could scale faster and with less clinical risk than franchising physical clinics.
