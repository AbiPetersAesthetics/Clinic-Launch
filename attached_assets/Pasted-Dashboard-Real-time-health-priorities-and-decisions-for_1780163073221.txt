Executive Summary
Yes — on the numbers, 9a Jewry Street is viable enough to justify active negotiation. The clinic only needs £7,631/month to break even, which is 18% occupancy, against a 17-day revenue ceiling of £43,010/month at 100% occupancy. That break-even threshold is low relative to the realistic case of £30,107/month revenue and £12,295/month net at 70% occupancy. Importantly, the corrected 17-day ceiling is still £43,010/month, so the old 22-day assumption does not reduce the modelled ceiling; the practical issue is not theoretical capacity, but whether Winchester can be built from a true cold start fast enough. The single biggest commercial risk is not rent — it is pricing and ramp execution in a market where APA is targeting premium pricing, often 1st or 2nd most expensive locally, while starting with zero transferred clients in Winchester. My recommendation is clear: proceed into negotiation on 9A this week, but do not commit unless you secure tenant-protective heads of terms, especially rent-free, a break option, and clarity on repair/service obligations. Financially, her £42,500 savings, £4,500/month nursing income and £10,000/month Bedhampton model income are enough to support the risk if the lease is structured properly.

Detailed Assessment
Financial
The three scenarios support the deal: even the conservative case at 30% occupancy still produces £2,884/month net, while the realistic case delivers £12,295/month net and the aggressive case £14,647/month net. With break-even revenue at just £7,631/month and break-even occupancy at 18%, the rent commitment is commercially supportable provided the Winchester ramp gets above a very modest occupancy threshold. Given this is a cold-start site with no Bedhampton client transfer, the realistic target is achievable over 12 months, but not if she insists on holding top-of-market pricing without enough launch marketing and social proof.

Property
At £2,708/month rent with no VAT on rent and no service charge, 9A is not cheap, but it is commercially clean and still healthy at 9% of realistic revenue, comfortably inside the usual <15% benchmark. The key heads of terms points are rent-free for fit-out and launch, a tenant break clause, confirmation of repairing obligations, and tight drafting around permitted use and alterations. She should push back hard on any full repairing liability without a schedule of condition, and avoid a long lease with no break given this is a first-site cold-start in a competitive premium market.

Market
Winchester absolutely has the demand profile for a premium medical aesthetics clinic: affluent ABC1 households, strong 35–60 female demographic, and a consumer base that values credentials over low prices. Jewry Street is a credible secondary city-centre pitch with lower occupancy cost than prime high street but still benefits from city footfall, destination visits and local awareness. Competitive risk is real, but the market is not saturated and there is no single dominant operator with overwhelming review depth and a full-menu lock on the city.

Competitor Analysis
The strongest visible competitors are Dr Victoria Clinic (0.4 miles, 4.8★, 132 reviews, doctor-led premium), Simply Skin Clinic (0.5 miles, 5★, 103 reviews, nurse-led natural-results), Wessex Skin (0.8 miles, 5★, 65 reviews, luxury medical/IP), and The Aesthetics Bae (1.4 miles, 5★, 143 reviews, trusted local destination). These operators prove demand and set a premium benchmark, but none appears to dominate the whole Winchester market with both massive review depth and complete category ownership. APA's edge is qualified nurse-led credibility and the ability to position as a medical practitioner rather than salon aesthetics, but she is vulnerable on local proof because Winchester starts from zero local client transfer. Her pricing is the key pressure point: anti-wrinkle 3 areas at £350 is 1st most expensive versus a £320 median, lip filler 0.5ml at £280 is 1st most expensive versus £200 median, Profhilo at £650 is 1st versus £550 median, and skin boosters at £300 are 1st versus £180 median. That is defensible only if presentation, reviews, clinical authority and outcomes are visibly superior from launch.

Demographics & Catchment
The catchment is strong enough to support this: roughly 47,000 in the city and 125,000 across the district, with household income around £52,000 and a heavy ABC1 professional base. The core buyer is the 35–60 female professional or business owner who is price-aware but prepared to pay more for a qualified medical practitioner, especially for injectables and regenerative treatments. Seasonal patterns fit aesthetics well — Jan, May–June and Oct–Nov should be the main growth accelerators, while Dec, Feb and Aug are softer and need to be budgeted for. Jewry Street is commercially sensible because it sits in a visible central zone without paying prime-pitch rents, which supports a premium-but-not-overhead-heavy model.

Strategic
Strategically this move makes sense: Bedhampton has already proven product-market fit, with £169,061 revenue to date, 61.6% repeat rate and average client spend of £215, so she is not testing whether the business model works. Bedhampton de-risks the expansion financially, but it also creates a dependency risk if she assumes that success will automatically replicate in a separate Winchester catchment.

Personal Finance
Her personal financial position supports taking this on: £42,500 savings, £4,500/month nursing income and £10,000/month Bedhampton model income give her real buffer against a slow first six months. Household need is only £4,166/month, so she is not relying on Winchester to pay her immediately.

Life Design
The nursing exit timing broadly aligns with a 1 November 2026 opening, but only just; any earlier loss of the £4,500/month nursing income would reduce her comfort buffer. Her clinic-day plan and model are broadly aligned, but family logistics remain unresolved: school-run cover is not specified, David's exact role is undocumented, and that creates operational fragility. The model states a 17-day correction while also confirming the £43,010 ceiling and 18% break-even occupancy; commercially, the bigger issue is not the ceiling but whether her actual diary discipline and childcare constraints slow Winchester's ramp.
